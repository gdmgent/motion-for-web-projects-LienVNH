The objective is to checkmate the opponent, which means putting their king under attack with no escape possible. Chess is a game of strategy, tactics, and foresight, where every move can influence the outcome of the game.

King

Moves one square in any direction.

Each player has one king, located next to the queen. The king can move one square in any direction. He is the most important piece and must be protected. If the king is checkmated, the player loses.

Queen

Moves any number of squares diagonally, horizontally, or vertically.

Each player has one queen, situated next to the king. The queen can move both straight and diagonally, any number of vacant squares. She is the most powerful piece in the game.

Rook

Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

Each player has two rooks, positioned in the corners of the board. Rooks move straight forward, backward, or sideways, any number of vacant squares. They can capture other pieces that obstruct their path.

Bishop

Moves any number of squares diagonally.

Each player has two bishops, positioned next to the knights. Bishops move diagonally across the board, any number of vacant squares. They always remain on the same color squares they started on.

Each player has two knights, located next to the rooks. Knights move in an 'L' shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicularly. They can jump over other pieces and are the only ones allowed to do so.

Pawn

Moves one square forward, but on its first move, it can move two squares forward. It captures diagonally one square forward.

Each player starts with eight pawns on the second rank of the board. Pawns move straight forward, one square at a time. However, on their first move, pawns have the option to move two squares forward. Pawns capture diagonally forward and can only eliminate other pieces in that manner.
